## Common Symptoms of Angioedema

Angioedema can present with a variety of symptoms depending on the affected area and the underlying cause. Here are the most common signs to watch for:

<details class="interactive-detail">
<summary>👤 Facial and Oral Symptoms</summary>

- Swelling of the lips, tongue, or cheeks
- Puffiness around the eyes or eyelids
- Difficulty speaking or swallowing if the tongue is affected
- Changes in facial appearance

</details>

<details class="interactive-detail">
<summary>🫁 Respiratory Symptoms</summary>

- Swelling of the throat or airways
- Difficulty breathing or shortness of breath
- Wheezing or stridor (high-pitched breathing sound)
- Feeling of tightness in the throat

</details>

<details class="interactive-detail">
<summary>👐 Skin and Extremity Symptoms</summary>

- Swelling of hands, feet, or genitals
- Hives or welts on the skin surface
- Itching or burning sensation
- Pain or discomfort in swollen areas

</details>

<details class="interactive-detail">
<summary>🚨 Emergency Warning Signs</summary>

**Seek immediate medical attention if you experience:**
- Rapid swelling of the face, lips, or tongue
- Difficulty breathing or swallowing
- Hoarse voice or trouble speaking
- Dizziness or fainting
- Severe abdominal pain (in some cases)

</details>

## When Symptoms Appear

Symptoms typically develop suddenly and can last from a few hours to several days. The swelling may come and go, and episodes can be triggered by various factors including:

- Food allergies
- Medications
- Stress
- Infections
- Hormonal changes

## Important Note

Not all swelling is angioedema. If you're experiencing persistent or unexplained swelling, consult a healthcare professional for proper diagnosis and treatment.
