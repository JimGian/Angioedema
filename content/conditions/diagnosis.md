## Diagnosis

Doctors diagnose angioedema by reviewing symptoms and checking for triggers or underlying causes.

<details class="interactive-detail">
<summary>🔬 Common tests and evaluations</summary>

- Medical history and symptom review
- Physical exam to identify swelling patterns
- Blood tests for immune markers or hereditary factors
- Imaging in rare cases when deeper tissue is involved

</details>

<details class="interactive-detail">
<summary>🧠 What doctors look for</summary>

- Sudden swelling without rash
- Recurrent episodes of skin or airway swelling
- Possible triggers such as medications, food, or stress
- Family history of hereditary angioedema

</details>

<details class="interactive-detail">
<summary>✨ When a diagnosis is urgent</summary>

- Swelling affects the throat or breathing
- Rapid onset of symptoms
- Recurrent unexplained angioedema
- Severe discomfort or pain

</details>
