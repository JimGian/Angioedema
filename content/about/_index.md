<div class="about-content">

  <h2>Our mission</h2>
  <p>Angioedema Info was created to fill a gap in accessible, plain-language medical education. We translate complex clinical information into short, accurate articles that focus on what matters most to patients and families dealing with angioedema.</p>

  <h2>What we cover</h2>
  <p>Our articles cover the full picture of angioedema — from what the condition is and how it is triggered, to how it is diagnosed and managed. Every topic is reviewed for accuracy and updated when guidelines change.</p>
  <ul>
    <li><strong>Types of angioedema</strong> — allergic, hereditary, ACE-inhibitor-induced, and idiopathic</li>
    <li><strong>Symptoms and emergency signs</strong> — knowing when to seek immediate help</li>
    <li><strong>Diagnostic workup</strong> — allergy testing, complement studies, and clinical history</li>
    <li><strong>Treatment options</strong> — antihistamines, corticosteroids, epinephrine, and biologics</li>
    <li><strong>Living with angioedema</strong> — trigger avoidance, action plans, and self-monitoring</li>
  </ul>

  <h2>Who this is for</h2>
  <p>This site is intended for:</p>
  <ul>
    <li>Patients recently diagnosed with angioedema who want to understand their condition</li>
    <li>Caregivers and family members supporting someone with angioedema</li>
    <li>Anyone who has experienced unexplained swelling and wants reliable background information</li>
  </ul>
  <p>It is not intended to replace a consultation with a qualified healthcare professional. If you are experiencing symptoms, please contact your doctor or visit an emergency department.</p>

  <h2>How to use this site</h2>
  <p>Start with the <a href="/Angioedema/conditions/angioedema/">What is Angioedema?</a> article if you are new to the condition. Then follow the natural flow through symptoms, diagnosis, and treatment. Use the contact page if you have a specific question we have not yet addressed.</p>
